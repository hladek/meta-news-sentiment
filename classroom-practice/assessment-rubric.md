# Assessment Rubric

Use this rubric to assess the quality of student metacognition during the activity.

| Criteria | 4 - Strong | 3 - Proficient | 2 - Developing | 1 - Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Prediction | Labels are thoughtful and sentence-specific. | Labels are mostly reasonable. | Labels are inconsistent or weakly connected to the text. | Labels are missing or mostly random. |
| Evidence | Uses precise words or phrases from the text as support. | Uses relevant but general evidence. | Uses limited evidence. | Gives little or no evidence. |
| Confidence monitoring | Confidence ratings match the strength of evidence. | Confidence ratings are mostly reasonable. | Confidence ratings are present but not well explained. | Confidence ratings are missing or careless. |
| Comparison with tool | Clearly identifies matches and disagreements. | Identifies most matches and disagreements. | Identifies some differences but with limited explanation. | Does not compare meaningfully. |
| Revision | Revises thinking using evidence and explains why. | Revises when appropriate with some explanation. | Revision is minimal or based mostly on the tool's authority. | Does not revise or explain. |
| Reflection | Clearly explains thinking strategies and next steps. | Explains at least one useful strategy. | Reflection is vague. | Reflection is missing or off task. |

## Optional Scoring

Add the six criteria for a score out of 24.

- 21-24: Advanced metacognitive control
- 16-20: Proficient metacognitive control
- 10-15: Developing metacognitive control
- 6-9: Beginning metacognitive control
